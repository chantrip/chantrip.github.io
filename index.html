About

I am an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science / Data Science & AI programs at the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT), Thailand. My work combines AI, data-intensive systems, and inclusive technologies to support equitable access to information and services, especially for deaf and hard-of-hearing communities in ASEAN.

I collaborate with partners across Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Japan, and beyond on building sign language corpora, translation systems, and AI-powered tools that can be deployed in real-world settings such as education, broadcasting, and public services.

Current interests
  • Large-scale sign language datasets and annotation workflows
  • Neural architectures for sign recognition and translation
  • LLM-augmented systems for accessible knowledge retrieval
  • Data engineering, MLOps, and trustworthy AI in practice
  • AI in education and technology for social good

Sign Language AI Projects

A major focus of my work is developing AI-based sign language technologies for ASEAN deaf communities. This includes building corpora, recognition systems, translation models, and deployment prototypes with partners in Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Japan.

Corpus & Datasets

ASEAN Sign Language Corpus

Cross-lingual sign language datasets capturing varieties such as Thai Sign Language (TSL) and Indonesian Sign Language (BISINDO), enabling training and benchmarking of modern models.

  • High-quality video recordings with gloss-level annotation
  • Integration with tools such as ELAN and keypoint extraction
  • Designed for research, education, and downstream applications
Models & Architectures

Sign Recognition & Translation

Exploring deep learning pipelines for isolated and continuous sign recognition, as well as sign-to-text translation, using state-of-the-art video and sequence models.

  • CNN/LSTM and transformer-based video models
  • Multimodal representations combining keypoints and raw video
  • Evaluation across multiple signers and language varieties
Applications

Accessible Services & Prototypes

Prototype systems that bring sign language AI into real-world environments, such as education and public information services.

  • Sign language support for digital signage and broadcasting
  • Interactive tools for learning and practicing sign language
  • Concepts for browser extensions and avatar-based translation

If you are interested in collaborating on sign language datasets, models, or deployment pilots in ASEAN, feel free to reach out.
